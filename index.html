About Me

I'm a long-time nerd who found his way into analytics and data science through an unexpected route: a middle school special education classroom. I grew up in Pasadena, CA, and earned dual degrees in Spanish Linguistics and Technical Theater at UC Berkeley; I mean, why pick just one thing? After college, I was clearly very hireable, but I joined Teach For America thinking it'd be a two-year detour before law school. Instead, I spent five years teaching at a Title I school, learning firsthand how systems and inequities show up for kids and educators every day. Somewhere between managing IEP meetings, developing RTI programs, and building spreadsheets to track behavior plans, I realized I cared as much about how we do the work as what work we're doing.

That interest in systems and tools led me to TFA's national data team, where I leveled up from Excel wizardry to full-blown BI and analytics work. Eventually I earned my MS in Analytics from Georgia Tech where I got to experiment with marketing data, manufacturing controls, financial modeling, and machine learning. These days, I lead a small but mighty Workforce Analytics team at KIPP SoCal, using data to improve how we support the adults who support our students. I live in Duarte, CA with my wife and our two dogs, think too much about bad charts I see in the wild, and try to never miss an LA Kings game. If there's a puzzle to solve — whether it's in a D&D campaign or a messy dataset — that's probably where you'll find me.
